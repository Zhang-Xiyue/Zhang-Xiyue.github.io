Overview: Computer-aided formal verification aims to improve the quality of digital systems by using logical reasoning, supported by automated software tools, to analyse their designs. The idea is to build a mathematical model of a system and then try to prove formal properties of it that validate the system's correctness, or at least that help discover subtle bugs. The proofs can be millions of lines long, so specially-designed computer algorithms are used to search for and check them. Properties are formalised as formulae in proper temporal logics.
